# Loan Agency KYC Process

## Overview

This skill covers the end-to-end KYC/AML process for loan agency operations. KYC is a prerequisite for deal closing — the deal cannot close until KYC is approved for all required parties.

**Who requires KYC:** Named borrowers, signing parties on escrows, sub-agency counterparties.

**Team roles:** RA initiates and coordinates → compliance team enters in KYC screening platform → client completes → system scores → approval or escalation.

---

## Critical Rules (Read First)

### 1. KYC Approval Is Valid for 30 Days Only

From the date of approval, KYC clearance is valid for **30 calendar days**. If the deal doesn't close within that window, a refresh is required.

### 2. Risk Scoring Determines Approval Path

| Risk Score | Approval Path |
|------------|--------------|
| **Low** | Auto-approved |
| **Medium** | Compliance review → may require additional documentation |
| **High** | Compliance + Legal review → enhanced due diligence |

### 3. CDD Requirements Remain in Force

While CTA/BOI reporting has been largely suspended for domestic companies, the FinCEN CDD Rule remains in force. KYC must still be performed.

## 1. Who Requires KYC

| Party | Required? |
|-------|----------|
| Named borrower(s) | Yes — always |
| Guarantors (signing parties) | Case-by-case |
| Escrow signing parties | Yes |
| Sub-agency counterparties | Yes |
| Lenders | No |
| Counsel | No |

---

## 2. KYC Template Completion

RA fills the KYC template with 17 data points:

1. Entity legal name
2. DBA / trade names
3. Entity type
4. Jurisdiction of formation
5. Principal place of business
6. Mailing address
7. Tax ID / EIN
8. Date of formation
9. Industry / business description
10. Website
11. Beneficial owners (25%+ ownership)
12. Control persons
13. Authorized signers
14. Source of funds
15. Expected transaction activity
16. Related entities / parent company
17. PEP status

---

## 3. Submission to Compliance

1. RA completes template
2. RA submits to compliance team
3. Compliance reviews for completeness
4. Compliance enters into KYC screening platform
5. Platform generates link for client

---

## 4. Screening Workflow

### Client Steps
1. Receives screening link
2. Completes questionnaire online
3. Uploads supporting documents
4. Submits

### Platform Processing
- Automated screening: sanctions lists, adverse media, PEP databases, public records
- Generates risk score: Low, Medium, or High

---

## 5. Approval Process

### Low Risk → Auto-approved. 30-day clock starts.

### Medium Risk
- Compliance review
- May request additional documentation
- Approval or escalation decision
- 30-day clock starts on approval

### High Risk
- Compliance + Legal review
- Enhanced due diligence may include: additional documentation, independent verification, source of wealth review
- Escalation: compliance → compliance manager → general counsel
- 30-day clock starts on approval

---

## 6. KYC Refresh

**When required:** Deal closing delayed beyond 30 days, material change in circumstances, periodic refresh per policy.

**Process:** RA notifies compliance → scope determined (minor re-screen vs full refresh) → new 30-day clock on re-approval.

---

## 7. Escalation Path

| Issue | Escalate To |
|-------|-------------|
| Medium risk | Compliance for review |
| High risk | Compliance + Legal |
| Sanctions hit | General Counsel immediately |
| Client unresponsive | RA → RM → deal counsel outreach |
| KYC blocking closing | RM → management (timeline) |
| Novel entity/jurisdiction | Compliance manager |

---

## 8. OFAC/Sanctions Screening (Ongoing)

KYC is not one-time for sanctions. Screening must occur: at onboarding, at each payment, whenever lists are updated.

**Penalties:** Up to $377,700 per violation (or 2x transaction). 10-year statute of limitations. 10-year recordkeeping.

**Cross-border:** Screen against US, EU, and UK regimes — programs may differ.

---

## 9. Timeline Planning

| Step | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Template completion | 1-2 BD |
| Compliance entry | 1-2 BD |
| Client completes questionnaire | 3-10 BD (bottleneck) |
| Platform scoring | 1-2 BD |
| Low risk approval | Same day |
| Medium risk review | 2-5 BD |
| High risk review | 5-15 BD |

**Total:** 7-20+ BD. Start as early as possible. Client responsiveness is the largest delay.
