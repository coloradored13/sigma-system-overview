## Loan Product Quick Reference

| Product | Amortization | Maturity | Typical Spread | Holders | Key Feature |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------------|---------|-------------|
| TLA | 5-10%/yr | 5-6 yr | SOFR+100-160 (IG) | Banks | Maintenance covenants, pro rata |
| TLB | 1%/yr (bullet) | 7 yr | SOFR+~341 | CLOs, institutions | Cov-lite (90%+), soft call 101 |
| Revolver | N/A (drawn/repaid) | 5 yr | 25-50 bps commitment fee | Banks | Springing covenant at 35-40% draw |
| DDTL | N/A until drawn | 7 yr | Ticking fee ~50-100% of margin | Institutions | Cannot reborrow once repaid |
| Unitranche | Varies | 5-7 yr | Higher than TLB | Private credit | Single tranche, AAL governs first-out/last-out |
| PIK | Capitalizes to principal | Varies | 50-200 bps premium | Private credit | ~14% of private credit includes PIK option |
| ABL | Varies | 3-5 yr | SOFR+150-250 | Banks | Borrowing base, field exams, advance rates |

## Operationally Critical Facts

- **90%+ of BSL is covenant-lite** — operational focus shifts to LME, amendments, restructurings
- **CLOs hold 66-69% of institutional loans** — single loan held by dozens of CLO vehicles with unique constraints
- **Third-party agents handle ~45-50% of settled trades** but post higher settlement times than banks
- **Private credit and BSL are converging** — terms, documentation, and investor base increasingly overlap
- **Soft call 101** standard for 6-12 months on TLB repricing
- **MFN cushion** typically 50 bps; sunset periods in only 31% of loans
- **ABL advance rates:** AR 70-90%, inventory 50-70%, equipment 25-40%
