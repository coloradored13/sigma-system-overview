# Quick Reference: Glossary & KB Guide

For the full 213-term glossary, see `Doc0_Glossary_and_KB_Preface.md`.

## Most-Used Terms

**Administrative Agent (AA):** Bank or third-party entity appointed under the credit agreement to act as agent for the lender syndicate. Handles payments, notices, amendment coordination, KYC. NOT a fiduciary — acts mechanically per the agreement.

**Collateral Agent:** Holds security interests (liens) on behalf of the secured parties. Manages UCC filings, pledged assets, DACAs, collateral release. May be the same entity as the AA or a separate appointment.

**Escrow Agent:** Holds funds in escrow pending satisfaction of release conditions (closing conditions, indemnity holdbacks, etc.).

**Pro Rata Share:** Each lender's proportionate share of commitments and outstanding loans. Must be recalculated after any balance change (assignments, repayments, PIK capitalization). Rounding errors compound.

**Required Lenders:** Typically lenders holding >50% of outstanding commitments. Can direct the agent on most actions. Excludes defaulting lenders from the calculation.

**Sacred Rights:** Provisions requiring unanimous or affected-lender consent (not just Required Lenders). Typically: extending maturity, reducing principal/interest, releasing all/substantially all collateral, changing pro rata sharing, changing Required Lender definition.

**Maker-Checker:** Two-person control on all system changes and wire approvals. Maker enters, checker reviews, approver (usually RM) signs off.

**Deal Summary:** The central operational artifact created by the RM after closing. TT uses it for system setup. If it's wrong, everything downstream breaks.

**DSN (Debt Service Notice):** Notice sent to lenders specifying interest/fees due, payment date, and wire instructions. Generated from the loan system after rate setting.

**Assignment & Assumption (A&A):** The legal document transferring a lender's position to a buyer. Requires agent processing, register update, and sometimes borrower/agent consent.

**DLX:** Loan administration system/platform used for deal setup, transaction processing, and position management.
