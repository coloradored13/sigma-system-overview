# Quick Reference: Credit Agreement Interpretation

For full case law, documentation trends, and analysis, see `Doc2_Credit_Agreement_Interpretation_Revised.md`.

## Agent-Critical Provisions

### Sacred Rights (Unanimous/Affected-Lender Consent Required)
These CANNOT be amended by Required Lenders alone:
- Extend maturity dates
- Reduce principal or interest rate
- Release all/substantially all collateral or guarantors
- Change pro rata sharing provisions
- Change Required Lender definition
- Subordinate liens or payment priority

### Amendment Voting Thresholds
| Action | US (LSTA) | UK/EU (LMA) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Standard amendments | >50% (Required Lenders) | 66⅔% (Majority Lenders) |
| Sacred rights | 100% or each affected lender | 100% or each affected lender |
| Snooze-you-lose | Agent may count non-responsive as consenting (some agreements) | Similar but less common |

### Erroneous Payment Rules (Post-Citibank/Revlon)
- Agent has sole discretion to determine if payment was erroneous
- Lenders must return funds within **2 business days** plus interest
- Payment without prepayment notice = **presumed error**, lender must notify agent within 1 BD
- Lenders waive discharge-for-value defense
- Agent has subrogation and set-off rights

### LME Blocker Provisions
- **Serta blockers** in ~71% of loans — prevent uptier exchanges via "open market purchase" loophole
- **J.Crew blockers** in ~39% of HY bonds — prevent IP/asset transfers to unrestricted subsidiaries
- Fifth Circuit (Dec 2024): "open market purchase" means purchase on the secondary market, not private negotiation

### EBITDA Add-Back Caps
- Present in **86% of deals**, typically **25% of EBITDA**
- Synergy look-forward: 12-24 months
- S&P study: adjusted EBITDA exceeds actual by ~30% on average over 2 years
- Key question: is cap calculated before or after add-backs? (post-adjustment is more borrower-favorable)

## LSTA vs LMA Key Differences

| Feature | LSTA (US) | LMA (UK/EU) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Transfer mechanism | Assignment | Novation (creates new contract) |
| Voting threshold | >50% | 66⅔% |
| Certain funds | No equivalent | Required for acquisition finance |
| Erroneous payment | Presumption of error + waiver | Simpler (English law favorable to agent) |
| Snooze-you-lose | Common | Less common |
| Yank-a-bank | Standard | Available but less standardized |
