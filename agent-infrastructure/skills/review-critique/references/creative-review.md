# Creative Review

Evaluation of work where quality is principle-based, not rule-based. The reviewer applies judgment informed by design principles, brand standards, and user needs — not a compliance checklist.

**Use for:** design critique, brand voice review, content review, UX copy review, visual design feedback, layout evaluation.

---

## Design Critique Framework

### 1. First Impression (2 seconds)
- What draws the eye first? Is that the right element?
- What's the emotional reaction?
- Is the purpose immediately clear?

### 2. Usability
- Can the user accomplish their goal?
- Is the navigation intuitive?
- Are interactive elements obvious?
- Are there unnecessary steps?

### 3. Visual Hierarchy
- Is there a clear reading order?
- Are the right elements emphasized?
- Is whitespace used effectively?
- Is typography creating the right hierarchy?

### 4. Consistency
- Does it follow the design system?
- Are spacing, colors, and typography consistent?
- Do similar elements behave similarly?

### 5. Accessibility (Quick Check)
- Color contrast (4.5:1 normal text, 3:1 large text)
- Touch target sizes (44x44 CSS pixels minimum)
- Text readability at default and zoomed
- Alt text on meaningful images

For full accessibility audit, see `references/standards-review.md`.

## Brand & Content Review Framework

### Voice and Tone
- Does the content match defined brand voice attributes?
- Is the tone appropriate for the content type and audience?
- Are there shifts in voice that feel inconsistent?
- Flag specific sentences that deviate, with explanation of why

### Messaging
- Are key messages present and clear?
- Is the value proposition stated early?
- Do claims have supporting evidence?
- Are CTAs clear and appropriately placed?

### Terminology and Style
- Product names used correctly and consistently?
- Industry terms used accurately?
- Acronyms defined on first use?
- Active voice preferred over passive?

### Legal/Compliance Flags (Content-Specific)
- Unsubstantiated claims ("fastest", "best", "#1")?
- Missing disclaimers or disclosures?
- Competitor comparisons that could be challenged?
- Testimonials without proper attribution?

## UX Copy Review Framework

### Clarity
- Can the user understand what to do from the copy alone?
- Are labels descriptive? ("Save changes" not "Submit")
- Are error messages specific? ("Email must include @" not "Invalid input")

### Tone
- Does the copy match the emotional context? (Error states need empathy, not humor)
- Is it consistent with the product's voice?

### Completeness
- Are all states covered? (Empty, loading, error, success, edge case)
- Are destructive actions clearly warned?
- Are next steps clear after completion?

## Creative Review Severity Guide

| Level | Creative Review Meaning |
|---|---|
| 🔴 Blocker | Fundamentally undermines the goal — confusing CTA, misleading content, broken hierarchy that prevents comprehension |
| 🟡 Issue | Noticeable quality gap — inconsistent spacing, off-brand tone, unclear labeling |
| 🔵 Suggestion | Polish opportunity — tighter copy, better visual rhythm, alternative approach worth considering |
| ✅ Strength | Effective element — call out to preserve and replicate |

## Gotchas

- **Match feedback to design stage.** Early wireframe = directional. Final comp = pixel-level.
- **"I don't like it" is not feedback.** Identify what's not working and why, connected to a principle or user need.
- **Review what was asked.** If they want feedback on the copy, don't redesign the layout.
- **Brand guidelines override personal taste.** If the brand is playful and you prefer formal, the brand wins.
