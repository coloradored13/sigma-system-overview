## Key Findings

### Where Upper-K Money Is Going (Empirical 2024-2026)

**Spending concentration at the top is structural and worsening.**

The top 10% of US households represent 49.7% of all consumer spending (BofA Institute Jan 2026), up from ~38% in 2010. In the 12 months to September 2024, top-10% household consumption grew 12% while middle and lower-income declined in absolute terms. Spending growth divergence: +2.5% YoY for higher-income vs. +1.0% middle vs. +0.3% lower-income (BofA Institute Jan 2026). Top 30% account for over 50% of out-of-pocket spending; bottom 50% account for less than 30%. |source: [independent-research:T2] BofA Institute Jan 2026, CONVERGED | VERIFIED

**The mechanism is income-stream-driven, not wealth-stock-driven (mandate #4).**

A genuine finding revision under R2 pressure: the K-shape in consumer spending is primarily driven by income-stream concentration at the top decile — wages, dividends (+46% since 2019), capital gains distributions, bonuses, and rental income (+54% since 2019, BEA/BofA). It is NOT primarily the wealth-stock (unrealized equity/home equity appreciation) that drives consumption.

The Federal Reserve FEDS Note (August 5, 2025, T1) establishes the mechanism: MPC out of wealth for the top-20% income earners = 0.8 cents per $1 of wealth gains; bottom-80% = 7.5 cents per $1 — a tenfold difference. Wealth concentration therefore mechanically REDUCES aggregate spending propensity, not increases it, because high-wealth households spend very little of each dollar of wealth gain. |source: [independent-research:T1] Fed FEDS Note Aug 2025 | HIGH

**Implication for K-fragility:** the K-shape fragility channel runs through the INCOME channel (capital gains drying up, bonus compression, dividend cuts), not through paper equity decline alone. A 20% equity correction without income compression has limited spending consequences for the top decile. A recession with income contraction is the true fragility signal. BELIEF[K-fragility-via-paper-equity-decline] = 0.35; BELIEF[K-fragility-via-recession-income-compression] = 0.70. |source: [independent-research:T1] EC DA[#8]
