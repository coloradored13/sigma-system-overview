# 3-Dimension Edge-Verification Framework

Meta-finding from R-2026-05-17-k-shape-opportunities: a structural tool for verifying whether an operator actually belongs in the high-probability reference class before acting on opportunity-viability beliefs. Attribution: [R-2026-05-17-k-shape-opportunities, 2026-05-17]

This page was mandated by RCA DA[#20] as a synthesis-mandate: "Without it, all opportunity-viability BELIEFs are 17pp-overcalibrated from user's current state."

---

## The Framework

Before acting on any opportunity-viability BELIEF from sigma-review analysis, the operator must self-verify their edge along three dimensions:

### Dimension 1: Prior Domain Expertise

**What it is:** Prior operational exposure to the target vertical and its customers. NOT general competence. NOT having read extensively about the space.

**What it requires:** You have worked in, with, or adjacent to the specific vertical being targeted. You understand the operational problems from the inside. Customers in the vertical would recognize your background as relevant.

**Why it matters:** The operator-edge premium (17pp above BLS baseline) derives substantially from domain expertise that creates genuine differentiation — the prior knowledge of what problems are actually painful, what solutions have been tried, and what the customer decision-making process looks like.

**The test:** Can you name 3 specific operational problems in this vertical that you encountered personally (not read about) and that a naive entrant would not know to look for?

---

### Dimension 2: Network Access to First 3-5 Customers

**What it is:** Pre-existing relationships capable of generating first paid engagements WITHOUT cold outreach. Not a contact list. Not LinkedIn connections. Not "I could reach them."

**What it requires:** You have specific named individuals in the target vertical who (a) trust your judgment, (b) have authority to approve a purchase or pilot, and (c) would take a meeting from you specifically — not from a cold outreach from a generic consultant.

**Why it matters:** The time-to-revenue estimates in the opportunity shapes assume non-cold-start. A solo operator without network access to first 3-5 customers faces a fundamentally different go-to-market problem — one that typically doubles or triples time-to-revenue and significantly increases capital requirements for the exploration phase.

**The test:** Can you name 3-5 specific individuals (not company names; specific people) who would take a meeting from you about this service offering within the next 30 days?

---

### Dimension 3: Capital Management Discipline and 18-36 Month Runway

**What it is:** The capacity to operate for 18-36 months without immediate revenue dependency, combined with the financial discipline to manage that runway.

**What it requires:**
- Liquid capital sufficient for 18-36 months of operating expenses (not revenue replacement — operating expenses including tool stack, outbound, insurance, professional development)
- A track record of personal financial discipline — not spending growth matching income growth
- A written budget/plan, not just "I could figure it out"

**Why it matters:** The Stanford SF Study reference class includes operators who were deliberate about capital management. Burning out at month 14 because of financial stress is a primary execution failure mode (PM[6]: 13% probability). Most opportunities that work take 18-24 months to reach stable cash flow.

**The test:** Can you sustain 18 months at zero revenue from this venture without materially changing your personal financial situation? Have you written down what that looks like?

---

## If Any Dimension Is Absent

**If any of the three dimensions fail the test:**

- The opportunity-viability BELIEFs from this review DO NOT APPLY at their stated values
- Default to BLS-anchored ~25% 3-year-profit-positive rather than the conditional 42%
- The 17pp operator-edge premium must be EARNED, not assumed
- Recalibrate opportunity evaluation from the BLS baseline, not the Stanford SF Study baseline

**The math:** Acting on 0.65 or 0.72 BELIEF scores when you are actually at 0.25 (BLS baseline) means you are making decisions with a 40-47pp systematic overestimate of your probability of success.

## Connection to PM[7]

PM[7] (base-rate substitution self-deception) is the highest-probability pre-mortem failure mode (21%) across the 8 scenarios analyzed in R-2026-05-17. The 3-dimension edge-verification is the structural mitigation for PM[7].

The failure mode is not that operators don't know about base rates — it's that they systematically assume they are in the high-probability reference class without verifying it. This framework creates a specific, operational check against that assumption.

**Early warning signals that PM[7] is materializing:**
- After 6 months, comparison cohort showing <40% profit positivity
- Edge (domain expertise + capital + network) less differentiated than presumed at launch
- Customer conversations taking longer to convert than initial network access suggested

## Cross-Review Applicability

This framework was synthesized from a specific review (R-2026-05-17-k-shape-opportunities) but is designed as a generalizable pre-deployment check for any sigma-review that produces opportunity-viability BELIEFs conditioned on operator capability.

**When to apply this framework:**
- Before acting on any opportunity-viability BELIEF score from sigma-review analysis
- When sigma-review synthesis assumes a specific operator capability class (e.g., Stanford SF Study class, verified domain expert, capital-deployable)
- When BELIEF scores appear high (>0.60) and the question is whether those BELIEFs apply to THIS specific operator

**What to do with the result:**
- Pass on all 3 dimensions → conditional BELIEFs apply. Proceed with planning.
- Fail on 1 dimension → BELIEFs may apply at reduced confidence. Identify the specific gap and either close it before capital deployment or recalibrate expectations.
- Fail on 2+ dimensions → Default to BLS baseline. Do NOT act on conditional BELIEFs. Build toward the dimensions you are missing before committing capital.
