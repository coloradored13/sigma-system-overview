# Trust Charter Regulatory Path
Last updated: 26.4.9 | Reviews: R17

## Summary
The trust charter path for a loan administration entrant involves multiple intersecting regulatory regimes: NH NDTC (national trust company charter via OCC), CRD6 (EU banking licensing with a 93-day grandfathering window as of April 2026), and the current OCC environment that is described as "most favorable in a decade" for new charter applicants. Timing decisions on charter and EU mandate contracting are both operationally load-bearing before mid-2026 milestones.

## Key Findings

### OCC Charter Environment
- OCC environment for new charter applications: "most favorable in a decade" — supported by T1 evidence: 12 CFR 5.20 and the Coinbase conditional charter precedent [R17, 26.4.9]
- Charter timeline estimate: 4-6 months (conditional on application quality and no novel precedent issues) — calibrated at P=65-75% [R17, 26.4.9]
- Note: original analysis bundle OCC claim retained without change after review — T1 evidence held up [R17, 26.4.9]

### CRD6 — EU Banking Licensing (TIME-SENSITIVE)
- CRD6 core banking licensing effective January 11, 2027 [R17, 26.4.9]
- Grandfathering cutoff: contracts entered before July 11, 2026 are grandfathered [R17, 26.4.9]
- As of April 9, 2026: 93 days remain to the grandfathering cutoff [R17, 26.4.9]
- If any new EU mandates are planned, they must be contracted before July 11, 2026 [R17, 26.4.9]
- XVERIFY-PARTIAL: exemption from CRD6 is plausible but fact-specific — not a blanket safe harbor [R17, 26.4.9]
- BELIEF[CRD6-exempt] = 0.68 — meaningful uncertainty remains [R17, 26.4.9]
- Member-state fragmentation risk is real: implementation variation across EU members [R17, 26.4.9]
- Source: regulatory-licensing-specialist F6 (T1)

### Basel III Re-Proposal (FAVORABLE DEVELOPMENT)
- Original analysis bundle language: "capital-neutral re-proposal expected early 2026" — this is superseded [R17, 26.4.9]
- Actual outcome: Basel III re-proposal issued March 19, 2026 — capital-REDUCING (~4.8-5.2% CET1 for large banks, ~7.8% for smaller institutions) [R17, 26.4.9]
- Comment period runs through June 18, 2026 [R17, 26.4.9]
- Implication: this is MORE favorable for private credit than the bundle predicted — private credit market tailwind is understated in original analysis [R17, 26.4.9]
- Note: partially offsets BDC capital formation stress [R17, 26.4.9]
- Source: regulatory-licensing-specialist F3 (T1)
