## Spending Concentration Data

Top 10% of US households represent **49.7% of all consumer spending** (BofA Institute Jan 2026), up from ~38% in 2010. In the 12 months to September 2024, top-10% household consumption grew 12% while middle and lower-income declined in absolute terms.

Three-tier spending growth divergence (BofA Institute Jan 2026):
- Higher-income: +2.5% YoY
- Middle-income: +1.0% YoY
- Lower-income: +0.3% YoY

Top 30% account for over 50% of out-of-pocket spending; bottom 50% account for less than 30%.

The "K-shape" has evolved into an **"E-shape"** at the detail level: three consecutive years of spending growth outpacing income growth for middle-income Americans as of January 2026.

Source: BofA Institute Consumer Checkpoint Oct-Dec 2025 (T1), BofA Institute Jan 2026 (T2), Fortune E-shaped Feb 2026 (T2). VERIFIED across independent sources.

## Causal Mechanism: Income-Stream-Driven, Not Wealth-Stock-Driven

**Key finding revision from R2 adversarial pressure:** The K-shape in consumer spending is primarily driven by income-stream concentration, not by paper wealth appreciation.

**Federal Reserve FEDS Note (August 5, 2025 — T1 source)** establishes the mechanism:
- MPC (marginal propensity to consume) out of wealth gains for top-20% income earners = **0.8 cents per $1**
- MPC for bottom-80% = **7.5 cents per $1** — a tenfold difference

Implication: wealth concentration mechanically REDUCES aggregate spending propensity, not increases it, because high-wealth households spend very little of each dollar of wealth gain. The spending engine for upper-K is flow income, not stock appreciation.

**Flow differential evidence (BEA/BofA data):**
- Dividends: +46% for high-income since 2019
- Rental income: +54% for high-income since 2019
- After-tax wage growth: +4.0% high-income vs +1.4% low-income YoY (BofA Institute Oct-Dec 2025 T1)

Source: Federal Reserve FEDS Note Aug 5, 2025 (T1); BEA/BofA Institute (T1). CONVERGED — macro-analyst and economics-analyst arrived at income-stream-driven mechanism via independent research paths. Cross-validated in R-2026-05-17.

## K-Shape Fragility Channel

**K-fragility runs through the income channel, not paper equity decline.**

- BELIEF[K-fragility-via-paper-equity-decline] = 0.35 — paper mark-to-market losses have limited consumption consequences for the top decile
- BELIEF[K-fragility-via-recession-income-compression] = 0.70 — capital gains drying up, bonus compression, dividend cuts, executive comp compression

A 20% equity correction without income compression has limited spending consequences for the top decile. A recession with income contraction is the true fragility signal.

## Alternative Analytical Frames (Noted Limitations of K-Shape)

The K-shape framework is the primary organizing frame for spending-power analysis, but carries MEDIUM-vulnerability as an organizing frame per 3-provider XVERIFY (GPT-5.4-Pro, Gemini-3.1-Pro, DeepSeek-v3.2) in R-2026-05-17.

Alternative frames that are valid for specific sub-questions:
- **Generational divide:** Boomers + Silent Gen hold ~62.2% of net worth vs ~10% for Millennials (Fed DFA Q3 2025 T1). Superior frame for longevity/healthcare demand, real estate dynamics, intergenerational wealth transfer.
- **Homeowner vs. renter divide:** Homeowners with pre-rate-hike mortgage lock-in + 40%+ equity gains vs. renters facing 20-30% rent increases 2020-2025. Cuts across K-framework.
- **Flow vs. stock distinction:** MPC evidence points to income-flow concentration, not wealth-stock, as the spending driver (see mechanism section above).

Synthesis uses K-shape as primary frame because it best maps to spending power for opportunity discovery — acknowledged as a simplification.
