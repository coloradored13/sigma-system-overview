## PM[7]: Base-Rate Substitution Self-Deception

**Probability: 21% (highest-probability pre-mortem failure mode** in R-2026-05-17 across all 8 scenarios)

Definition: The operator assumes they are in the Stanford-SF-Study class (42% conditional probability) when they are actually in the BLS-all-startup class (~25%). They act on opportunity-viability beliefs that are 17pp overcalibrated from their actual position.

**Early warning signals:**
- After 6 months, comparison cohort showing <40% profit positivity
- Edge (domain expertise + capital + network) less differentiated than presumed at launch

**Mechanism:** All opportunity-viability BELIEF scores in this review (e.g., 0.65 for Shape E, 0.72 for Shape A at launch) are CONDITIONAL on the operator being in the Stanford-SF-Study class. If the 3-dimension edge-verification fails on any dimension, the unconditional estimate is ~0.25, not the conditional estimates cited throughout the analysis.

**Mitigation:** Interrogate "What makes me Stanford-SF-Study class rather than BLS-all-startup class?" BEFORE capital deployment. The 3-dimension edge-verification is the structural answer to this question.
